# ACL 2026 — Critic Expectations (compact)

Numbers below are corpus medians from 267 accepted orals. Use them to flag drafts that deviate *and provide no domain reason*. A deviation is not automatically wrong.

## Minimum structural skeleton an ACL draft should show
- Abstract (typically 5–10 sentences, 100–200 words)
- Introduction with an explicit contribution enumeration (a minority of orals do)
- Related Work (typical ~298 words)
- Method block — equation density varies heavily by task; many ACL methods are prompt-based or pipeline-based with 4 equation environments (median).
- Experiments with ≥8 tables and an ablation OR analysis section that isolates design choices.
- Conclusion

## What the corpus flags as reviewer-style red flags
- Missing or stub 'Ablation'/'Analysis' subsection — ACL papers isolate design choices slightly less often than CVPR (~44% have an explicit Ablation heading), but the analysis duty does not vanish; if no section isolates the contributions, reviewers will ask. Frame the ablation around which design choice drove the gain.
- SOTA claims without a paired quantitative result table reference — superlative mentions median 9 per paper; each should map to a table or figure.
- Citation drift: if your draft's NLP-family (ACL/EMNLP/NAACL/COLING/TACL/Findings) cite share is far below the corpus median (~8.3%), verify you are anchoring in the venue's prior work. ACL authors lean heavily on arXiv and ML conferences, so a near-zero NLP-fraction in 2026 is a strong signal the paper is grounded in ML rather than NLP — fine if intentional, suspicious if submitted to ACL. Cite-share is a heuristic, not a rule.
