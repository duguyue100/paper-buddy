# ICML 2026 — Critic Expectations (compact)

Numbers below are corpus medians from 303 accepted spotlights with arXiv sources. Use them to flag drafts that deviate *and provide no domain reason*. A deviation is not automatically wrong.

## Minimum structural skeleton an ICML draft should show
- Abstract (typically 7-8 sentences, 161-185 words)
- Introduction with an explicit contribution enumeration (36.3% of spotlights do)
- Related Work (typical ~347 words)
- Method block with formalism (typical ~17 equation environments — ICML is theory-heavy)
- Experiments with ≥5 tables and an ablation study
- Conclusion

## What the corpus flags as reviewer-style red flags
- Missing or stub 'Ablation' subsection — 43.2% of spotlights have an explicit Ablation heading; a missing one will be flagged.
- Abstract containing an enumeration of contributions (rare in ICML); the contribution list belongs in the intro, not the abstract.
- SOTA claims without a paired quantitative result table reference — superlative mentions median 8 per paper, every one should map to a table or figure (the `inspect.py` script flags unanchored superlatives directly).
- Citation drift: if your draft's ML-family (NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR/COLT/AISTATS/AAAI...) cite share is far below the corpus per-paper median (~26.2%), verify you are anchoring in the ML community's prior work. (ICML authors draw broadly — preprints take a large share too; the CV-family share median 0.0% is naturally lower for ICML than for a CVPR paper.)
