# CVPR 2026 — Critic Expectations (compact)

Numbers below are corpus medians from 106 accepted orals. Use them to flag drafts that deviate *and provide no domain reason*. A deviation is not automatically wrong.

## Minimum structural skeleton a CVPR draft should show
- Abstract (typically 5–10 sentences, 120–220 words)
- Introduction with an explicit contribution enumeration (about half of orals do)
- Related Work (typical ~393 words)
- Method block with formalism (typical ~11 equation environments)
- Experiments with ≥4 tables and an ablation study
- Conclusion

## What the corpus flags as reviewer-style red flags
- Missing or stub 'Ablation' subsection — even if your field uses fewer, you must isolate which design choice drove the gain; framing is observed in nearly every oral.
- Abstract containing an enumeration of contributions (rare — only ~2%); the contribution list belongs in the intro, not the abstract.
- SOTA claims without a paired quantitative result table reference — superlative mentions median 10 per paper, every one should map to a table or figure.
- Citation drift: if your draft's CV-family (CVPR/ICCV/ECCV) cite share is far below the corpus median (~23.0%), verify you are anchoring in the venue's prior work; cite-share is an heuristic, not a rule.
